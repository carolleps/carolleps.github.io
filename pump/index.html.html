Monday
Glutes & Lower Body
7 exercises · Rest 60–90 sec between sets · Start with 5 min light skipping
01 / Monday
Barbell Hip Thrust
4 × 12 reps
Glutes ★ Hamstrings Core
+
How to do it
  • 1 Sit on the floor with your upper back against the edge of a bench. Roll the barbell over your hips — use a pad to protect your hip bones.
  • 2 Plant both feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart, knees at ~90°. Grip the bar lightly for stability.
  • 3 Drive your hips explosively upward until your torso is parallel to the floor — like a tabletop. Chin tucked slightly.
  • 4 Squeeze your glutes hard at the top for 1 second. Lower slowly with control.
⭐ THIS is your primary lift — make it the one you track and add weight to each week. Even 2.5kg extra per session compounds fast.
02 / Monday
Barbell Back Squat
3 × 10 reps
Quads ★ Glutes ★ Hamstrings
+
How to do it
  • 1 Position the bar across your upper traps (not your neck). Step back, feet shoulder-width, toes angled slightly out.
  • 2 Take a deep breath, brace your core as if expecting a punch. Keep your chest tall.
  • 3 Hinge hips back and bend knees simultaneously. Lower until thighs are parallel to floor or below — depth is key.
  • 4 Drive through your entire foot to stand, exhaling at the top. Repeat.
💡 Start light — form and depth matter far more than weight at the start. Film yourself side-on to check your depth honestly.
03 / Monday
Romanian Deadlift
3 × 12 reps
Hamstrings ★ Glutes ★ Lower Back
+
How to do it
  • 1 Stand holding barbell or dumbbells in front of thighs, shoulder-width grip. Soft bend in the knees — this is not a squat.
  • 2 Push your hips back (like you're trying to touch a wall behind you). Keep the bar/weights close to your legs as they lower.
  • 3 Lower until you feel a deep stretch in your hamstrings — usually just below the knee. Spine stays neutral throughout.
  • 4 Drive hips forward to return to standing. Squeeze glutes at the top.
💡 This is NOT a squat — legs stay mostly straight. Think "hip hinge," not "knee bend." Feel the hamstring stretch guiding your depth.
04 / Monday
Walking Lunges
3 × 12 each leg
Glutes ★ Quads ★ Hamstrings
+
How to do it
  • 1 Hold dumbbells at your sides. Stand tall with core braced.
  • 2 Step one foot forward and lower your back knee toward the floor — don't let it slam down.
  • 3 Front shin stays vertical. Don't let your front knee cave in.
  • 4 Push through your front heel to stand, bringing your back foot forward into the next step. Alternate legs.
💡 Keep your torso perfectly upright — leaning forward shifts the work away from your glutes. Do these in a hallway or loop around your space.
05 / Monday
Banded Lateral Walks
3 × 15 each direction
Glute Medius ★ Hip Abductors Glutes
+
How to do it
  • 1 Place the resistance band just above your knees. Feet shoulder-width apart — enough that the band already has tension.
  • 2 Drop into a slight squat position — slight forward hinge, knees soft. Stay in this position the entire set.
  • 3 Step sideways, leading with one foot, then bring the other foot in — but don't let them come all the way together or you lose tension.
  • 4 15 steps right, 15 steps left = 1 set. Keep the knees pushing slightly outward against the band throughout.
💡 The moment the band goes slack, the outer glute stops working. Stay wide — maintain tension on every single step.
06 / Monday
Glute Kickbacks
3 × 15 each leg
Glutes ★★ Hamstrings
+
How to do it
  • 1 Loop the band around the rack at ankle height. Stand facing the rack, loop one ankle through the band.
  • 2 Rest hands lightly on the rack for balance. Slight forward lean at the hips.
  • 3 Keeping core tight, kick the banded leg straight back and slightly up. Squeeze your glute hard at the peak.
  • 4 Return slowly with control — don't let the band yank your leg forward. Complete all reps, then switch legs.
💡 Don't let your lower back arch at the top — the glute does the work, not the spine. Think "squeeze glute, not back."
07 / Monday
Plank Hold
3 × 30–45 sec
Core ★ Shoulders Glutes
+
How to do it
  • 1 Place forearms on the floor, elbows under shoulders. Rise onto your toes so only forearms and toes touch the floor.
  • 2 Squeeze everything simultaneously: glutes, quads, abs. Your body should be a rigid plank — no sagging hips, no raised bum.
  • 3 Eyes look down at the floor to keep your neck in a neutral position.
  • 4 Breathe steadily. Hold for the target time. Lower with control.
💡 Squeezing your glutes AND quads (not just your abs) makes this dramatically more effective — and harder. Build toward 60 seconds over time.
⚠️ Heart Rate Note: Alenami mildly raises your resting heart rate. Exercise will feel harder than usual — this is normal. Keep intensity moderate. If your heart is racing uncomfortably, you feel dizzy, or have chest tightness: stop and rest. Build gradually over your first 2–3 weeks.
Wednesday
Upper Body & Core
9 exercises · Moderate weights, full range of motion · Last 2 reps should feel challenging
01 / Wednesday
Dumbbell Shoulder Press
3 × 12 reps
Deltoids ★ Triceps Core
+
How to do it
  • 1 Sit or stand holding dumbbells at shoulder height, palms facing forward, elbows at ~90°.
  • 2 Brace your core and press both dumbbells directly overhead in a slight arc until arms are nearly straight.
  • 3 Don't lock elbows hard at the top. Lower slowly and with control back to the start position.
  • 4 Keep your lower back from arching — if seated, this is easier to control.
💡 Press straight up over your shoulders — not forward. Imagine you're pressing toward the ceiling directly above your head.
02 / Wednesday
Dumbbell Bent-Over Row
3 × 12 reps
Back ★ Rhomboids Biceps
+
How to do it
  • 1 Hinge at the hips with a flat back, leaning forward ~45°. Hold dumbbells hanging straight down, palms facing each other.
  • 2 Pull both elbows up and back toward your hips — not toward your ears.
  • 3 Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top for 1 second.
  • 4 Lower slowly and with full control — don't let gravity do the work on the way down.
💡 Think "elbows to hips" — this keeps the focus on your mid-back. Essential for desk workers: this exercise directly counteracts forward posture.
03 / Wednesday
Floor Chest Press
3 × 10 reps
Chest ★ Triceps Front Deltoids
+
How to do it
  • 1 Lie on your back on the floor, knees bent, feet flat. Hold dumbbells at chest height, elbows resting on the floor.
  • 2 Press both dumbbells straight up until arms are fully extended above your chest.
  • 3 Lower slowly until your upper arms touch the floor again — this is your natural stop point.
  • 4 Press back up immediately after touching. Breathe out on the press.
💡 The floor acts as a natural safety stop — you can use heavier dumbbells with more confidence than a bench. A great beginner-friendly variation.
04 / Wednesday
Lateral Raises
3 × 15 reps
Side Deltoids ★ Traps
+
How to do it
  • 1 Stand holding very light dumbbells at your sides, palms facing your body. Soft bend in the elbows.
  • 2 Raise both arms directly out to the sides simultaneously until they're parallel to the floor — forming a T shape.
  • 3 Hold for 1 second at the top. Then lower on a 3-count — slowly.
  • 4 Keep your torso still throughout — if you're swinging your hips, the weight is too heavy.
💡 Go lighter than you think. 4–6kg is usually the right weight for most beginners. The slow descent is where the muscle-building actually happens.
05 / Wednesday
Face Pulls with Band
3 × 15 reps
Rear Deltoids ★ Upper Back ★ Rotator Cuff
+
How to do it
  • 1 Loop band at head height on the rack. Hold both ends with an overhand grip. Step back until there's tension in the band.
  • 2 Pull the band toward your face, flaring your elbows wide and high — think "double bicep pose" as you pull.
  • 3 At the peak, your hands should be at ear level and elbows pointing out to the sides. Hold 1 second.
  • 4 Return slowly with full control. The band should be under tension even at full extension.
💡 This is the single best posture exercise in this program. It directly counteracts desk-worker hunching. Do it every session without exception.
06 / Wednesday
Dumbbell Bicep Curl
3 × 12 reps
Biceps ★ Forearms
+
How to do it
  • 1 Stand holding dumbbells at your sides, palms facing forward, arms fully extended. Keep upper arms pinned to your sides.
  • 2 Curl both dumbbells up toward your shoulders, rotating palms upward as you lift.
  • 3 Squeeze at the top for 1 second. Then lower with complete control — don't drop them.
  • 4 Reach full extension at the bottom every single rep — don't shortcut the range of motion.
💡 No swinging. If your body sways to lift the weight, it's too heavy. Drop down 2kg — controlled reps build far more than heavy sloppy ones.
07 / Wednesday
Tricep Overhead Extension
3 × 12 reps
Triceps ★★ Shoulders
+
How to do it
  • 1 Stand or sit holding one dumbbell with both hands overhead. Arms straight, gripping the top plate with thumbs hooked around the handle.
  • 2 Keeping your upper arms locked in place close to your head, bend at the elbows to lower the dumbbell behind your head.
  • 3 Lower until you feel a deep stretch in your triceps. Elbows point forward, close to your head throughout.
  • 4 Press back up to full arm extension. Breathe out at the top.
💡 Targets the back of the upper arm — a very common goal for women. Keep your core braced and ribs down — it's easy to arch your back as you press up.
08 / Wednesday
Dead Bug
3 × 8 each side
Deep Core ★ Lower Back Hip Flexors
+
How to do it
  • 1 Lie on your back, arms straight up toward ceiling, knees bent at 90° and lifted (like a table-top).
  • 2 Inhale. As you exhale, simultaneously lower your RIGHT arm behind your head AND your LEFT leg toward the floor.
  • 3 Stop just BEFORE your arm and leg touch the floor. Breathe in. Return to start.
  • 4 Repeat on the opposite side. That's one rep each side. Move slowly — this is not a speed exercise.
💡 Your lower back must stay flat on the mat the ENTIRE time. If it lifts off, you've gone too far — shorten your range of motion until it stays down.
09 / Wednesday
Side Plank
3 × 20–30 sec each side
Obliques ★ Hip Abductors Core
+
How to do it
  • 1 Lie on your side with your elbow directly under your shoulder. Stack both feet on top of each other.
  • 2 Lift your hips off the floor until your body forms a straight diagonal line from head to foot.
  • 3 Hold — breathe steadily. Your hips should NOT drop or pike up. Top arm can rest on hip or reach up.
  • 4 Lower with control. Switch sides and repeat.
💡 To progress: try adding hip dips (slowly lower your hip toward the floor and raise back up) or lifting your top leg while holding the plank.
Friday
Full Body with Glute Focus
8 exercises · Compound movements + glute finisher · End the week strong
01 / Friday
Barbell Deadlift
4 × 8 reps
Full Body ★★ Glutes ★ Hamstrings ★ Back
+
How to do it
  • 1 Stand with the bar over your mid-foot. Hinge at the hips to grip the bar just outside your shins. Hips higher than knees, shoulders above hips.
  • 2 Take a deep breath and brace your core hard — like you're about to get punched. Chest up, back flat.
  • 3 Drive the floor away with your legs. Once the bar passes your knees, drive your hips forward to stand tall. Bar stays close to your body throughout.
  • 4 To lower: hinge at the hips first, then bend the knees when the bar passes them. Controlled descent.
⭐ The most effective full-body exercise available to you. Start light and nail the form — the rewards compound significantly as you add weight over weeks and months.
02 / Friday
Barbell Hip Thrust
3 × 15 reps
Glutes ★★ Hamstrings
+
How to do it
  • 1 Upper back on bench, barbell across hips with pad. Feet flat, hip-width, knees at 90°.
  • 2 Drive hips explosively upward to parallel. Full squeeze at the top for 1 second.
  • 3 Lower slowly with control. Don't let your hips drop fast — the controlled descent counts.
  • 4 Higher rep target today (15) — go slightly lighter than Monday if needed, but hit all the reps.
💡 This is your second hip thrust session this week — glutes respond well to volume. Slightly lighter than Monday's weight is fine; the higher reps are the point.
03 / Friday
Goblet Squat
3 × 12 reps
Quads ★ Glutes ★ Core
+
How to do it
  • 1 Hold one heavy dumbbell vertically at your chest, cupping the top with both hands. Feet shoulder-width, toes angled slightly out.
  • 2 Squat deep — the weight at your chest acts as a counterbalance, making it easier to stay upright and go deeper.
  • 3 Keep your chest tall and knees tracking over your toes throughout. Elbows can lightly touch the inside of your knees at the bottom.
  • 4 Drive through your heels to stand. Breathe out at the top.
💡 One of the best squat variations for learning proper mechanics. The counterweight makes it much easier to maintain an upright chest and squat deep simultaneously.
04 / Friday
Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift
3 × 10 each leg
Hamstrings ★ Glutes ★ Balance
+
How to do it
  • 1 Stand on one leg, holding a dumbbell in the OPPOSITE hand (left leg = right hand dumbbell).
  • 2 Keeping your standing leg mostly straight, hinge at the hip and let your non-standing leg float back behind you for balance.
  • 3 Lower the dumbbell toward the floor — as far as your hamstring stretch allows. Keep hips square (don't let one side rotate open).
  • 4 Drive through the heel of your standing foot and squeeze your glute to return to standing. Complete all reps, then switch legs.
💡 This will expose imbalances between sides — don't be alarmed if one is weaker. It corrects itself surprisingly quickly. Use a wall for balance if needed.
05 / Friday
Dumbbell Step-Ups
3 × 12 each leg
Glutes ★ Quads ★ Balance
+
How to do it
  • 1 Hold dumbbells at your sides. Place your full foot on a bench or sturdy box — not just your toes.
  • 2 Drive through the HEEL of your working foot to stand on top of the bench. Your trailing leg should not push off the floor.
  • 3 Stand fully at the top, then slowly lower the trailing leg back down — controlled descent.
  • 4 Complete all reps on one leg before switching. This keeps more tension on the working glute.
💡 Drive through the HEEL — this is everything. If you push through your toes, it becomes a quad exercise instead of a glute exercise. Feel the difference consciously.
06 / Friday
Push-Ups
3 × Max Reps
Chest ★ Triceps ★ Shoulders Core
+
How to do it
  • 1 Hands slightly wider than shoulders, fingers forward or slightly out. Body in a rigid straight line from head to toes.
  • 2 Lower your chest toward the floor, keeping elbows at roughly 45° to your body — not flaring straight out.
  • 3 Lower until your chest nearly touches the floor (full range of motion). Press back up until arms are straight.
  • 4 Stop when form breaks down — that's your max for that set. Record your reps and aim to beat it next week.
💡 Start on your knees if needed — there is zero shame in it. 3 perfect knee push-ups beats 10 sloppy full ones. Full push-ups typically come within 4–6 weeks of consistent training.
07 / Friday
Russian Twist
3 × 12 each side
Obliques ★ Core ★ Hip Flexors
+
How to do it
  • 1 Sit on the floor with knees bent. Lean back slightly (about 45°) until your abs engage. Hold a dumbbell with both hands at your chest.
  • 2 Lift your feet slightly off the floor (harder) or keep them grounded (easier to start).
  • 3 Rotate your torso to the RIGHT, lowering the dumbbell toward the floor beside your right hip. Return to center. Rotate LEFT. That's one rep each side.
  • 4 Keep the movement slow and controlled — this is not a speed exercise.
💡 The rotation comes from your torso, not just your arms. Think of your arms following your chest as your ribcage turns. Controlled = more oblique activation.
08 / Friday
Glute Bridge Hold
3 × 30 sec hold
Glutes ★★ Hamstrings Core
+
How to do it
  • 1 Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat on the floor hip-width apart. Arms by your sides, palms down.
  • 2 Drive your hips up until your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees.
  • 3 Squeeze your glutes as hard as you possibly can. Hold this squeeze for the full 30 seconds — breathe steadily.
  • 4 Lower slowly after the hold. Rest briefly. Repeat for 3 sets.
🔥 This is your burnout finisher — your glutes should be burning by now. That burn means the work is happening. Add a dumbbell on your hips as you get stronger.
🔥 Week complete! Three sessions done — this is what consistent progressive overload looks like. Try to add a small amount of weight or one extra rep somewhere each week. That's how transformation happens.
