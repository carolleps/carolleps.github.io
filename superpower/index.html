My Cultural
Superpower

A step-by-step guide to writing your very first speech. Let's do this together! 💪

🌏✨🎤
Let's get started →
Step 1

What is a Cultural Superpower? 🤔

Before you write your speech, let's make sure you know what you're talking about. And it's actually pretty cool!

Your cultural superpower is NOT just about where your parents were born. It comes from all the different people, places and experiences that have shaped who you are:

🏠

The country you were born in

👨‍👩‍👧

Where your parents came from

👫

Friends from different backgrounds

✈️

Places you've visited or heard about

🍜

Foods, music and celebrations you've tried

💬

Conversations that changed how you see things

🌟

When you grow up surrounded by many different cultures, something amazing happens. You start to see the world in a bigger, kinder, more curious way. That is your superpower — and you already have it.

Step 2

Think Before You Write ✏️

Grab a piece of paper and answer these questions in your own words. There are NO wrong answers!

🌍 About the people and places that shaped you
  • 📍Where were you born? Where were your parents born?
  • 👥Do you have friends or family from different countries or backgrounds?
  • 🍛Have you ever tried food, music or celebrations from a culture different to yours?
  • 💡Has someone ever told you about their country and it surprised you?
  • 🗺️Have you ever visited a different place and noticed things were done differently?
💭 About what all that taught you
  • 🔍Has knowing different people made you more curious about the world?
  • 🤝Has it made you more understanding when someone seems different from you?
  • 🌐Did you ever realise that your way of doing things is just YOUR way — not the only way?
  • 🔭Do you feel like the world is much bigger than just your street or your school?
Step 3

Pick Your Superpower ⚡

Click the ones that feel true for you! Your speech can be about one of these — or a mix. It just has to be honest and real.

💛
Kinder
I've learned that everyone has their own story, so I'm more understanding
🌏
More Aware
I know the world is much bigger than my neighbourhood
🧠
More Knowledgeable
I know things about other places, foods, languages, and ways of life
🤝
More Understanding
I don't judge people just because they are different from me
🔭

I know I'm not the centre of the universe — and that's a cool thing to know
✨
More Curious
Instead of being nervous about differences, I'm curious and excited
Great choices! 🎉 Now use these to write the heart of your speech — Part 3!
Step 4

Build Your Speech 🏗️

Your speech has 4 parts. Each part only needs 2 to 4 sentences. Click each part to open it!

1
🪝 The Hook
Grab everyone's attention right from the start
▼

✏️ Write ONE great opening sentence in your own words. Make people want to keep listening!

  • "What if knowing people from different countries could actually change who you are as a person?"
  • "My superpower didn't come from a comic book. It came from the people around me."
  • "I think growing up around different cultures was one of the best things that ever happened to me."
2
🌺 Where It Comes From
Tell the audience about YOUR mix of experiences
▼

✏️ Tell the audience your personal mix. Where you were born, where your family is from, and the friends and experiences that shaped you. Pick the ones that feel most important!

💡 Example to inspire you — use your own words!
"I was born in Australia, but my family comes from Brazil. At school, my best friends are from the Philippines, Lebanon, and China. I've tried food I can't even pronounce, heard languages I don't understand, and learned that people celebrate the same things — like love and family — in completely different ways."
3
⚡ My Cultural Superpower
The heart of your speech — what did all of that give YOU?
▼

✏️ What kind of person has growing up in a multicultural world made you? Give a real example or a small story!

💡 Example to inspire you — use your own words!
"Because I've grown up around so many different people, I've learned that my way of doing things isn't the only way — and that's actually exciting, not scary. When I meet someone new who seems different from me, I'm curious instead of nervous. I've realised the world is so much bigger than my street, and I want to know as much of it as I can. That feeling — that curiosity and kindness — is my cultural superpower."
4
🎤 The Big Finish
Leave the audience with something to think about
▼

✏️ Write 2–3 sentences that feel strong and confident. Say them out loud — if they give you a little shiver, you've got it right! 😊

  • "Every person I've met from a different culture has added something to who I am. I think that's the best kind of growing up."
  • "Multiculturalism didn't just teach me about other people. It taught me about myself."
  • "The world is bigger than my neighbourhood. And because of the people around me, I already know a little bit of it."
Step 5

Check Your Speech ✅

Once you've written your draft, go through this list. Tick each one as you go!

0 / 7
✓
Does my first sentence make people want to keep listening?
✓
Did I explain where my mix of experiences comes from?
✓
Did I clearly describe my superpower — what it is and how I got it?
✓
Did I give a real example or a small story?
✓
Does my ending feel strong and leave people thinking?
✓
Is it between 1 and 2 minutes when I read it out loud? (about 150–200 words)
✓
Have I read it out loud at least once to practise?
Step 6

Delivering Your Speech 🎙️

Writing it is the hard part — speaking it is the fun part! Here's how to nail it.

🐢
Slow Down
When we're nervous we speak too fast. Breathe, take your time, and let each sentence land.
👀
Look Up
Look at the audience sometimes — you don't have to read every word off the page.
😌
Take a Breath
Before you start, take one big breath. It calms your nerves and gets you ready.
🗣️
Your Real Voice
You don't need a fancy voice — just your real, clear one. Speak like you're telling a friend.
⏸️
Pause After Line 1
After your opening sentence, pause for a second. Let it land before you keep going.
💛
They're On Your Side
Everyone in the room wants you to do well. They are cheering for you — even quietly!
🚀

You Have a Superpower.

Not everyone gets to grow up in a world full of different people, languages, foods, and stories. You did. And it made you more curious, more kind, and more aware. Now go write about it — in your own words, your own way.
